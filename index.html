Prepositions


Convention/Grammar

Prepositions are short words that show the relationship between other words in a sentence.

Prepositions of Time: These prepositions indicate when something happens.

Prepositions of Place: These show where something is or where an action happens.

Prepositions of Direction: These indicate movement or direction.

Prepositions of Manner: These describe how an action is performed.

Prepositions indicate location, time, direction, or other connections. Common prepositions include in, on, at, by, with, from, to, and many others.

The prepositional phrase functions as a single unit, providing additional details about the noun or pronoun it modifies.



Words that I learnt from the story

Scornfully: In a way that shows or expresses contempt or disrespect; with disdain.
Sentence: The bully looked scornfully at the smaller child's drawing, making fun of it.

Mused: To think about something carefully and for a long time; to ponder or reflect.
Sentence: As she walked in the park, Sarah mused about her plans for the summer vacation.

Pail: A cylindrical container with a handle, typically made of metal or plastic, used for carrying liquids or other substances.
Sentence: Jack and Jill went up the hill to fetch a pail of water.
